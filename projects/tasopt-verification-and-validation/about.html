Validating TASOPT against real aircraft geometry and performance, starting with the Boeing 737-800 and extending to regional and wide-body aircraft.

This project uses TASOPT, a medium-fidelity aircraft design and optimization tool, and tests whether it can reproduce real aircraft behavior. The first target is the Boeing 737-800: matching not just published geometry, but also payload-range behavior through a multi-mission approach.

What I’m building

  • A Boeing 737-800 reference model in TASOPT.
  • Geometry validation against real aircraft dimensions and published data.
  • Performance validation against a real payload-range diagram.
  • A multi-mission validation approach rather than relying on a single design point.
  • A repeatable V&V workflow for additional aircraft classes.

Why it matters

Before TASOPT can be used confidently for future aircraft concepts or environmental optimization, it needs to reproduce current aircraft with known geometry and performance. A rigorous V&V process helps separate modeling assumptions from real aircraft behavior.

Next validation targets

  • Regional aircraft: Embraer E195.
  • Wide-body aircraft: Boeing 777-300ER.
  • Turn the B738 process into a reusable pipeline for aircraft-level V&V across size classes.
